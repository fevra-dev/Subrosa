# Strictest-Regime-Wins Default Matrix ("design-to-this" floor)

**Derived 2026-07-04 from schema v1.0 Tier-1 records** (`ca-pipeda-law25`, `eu-gdpr-uk`, `us-ca-ccpa`); **re-derived same day after the Tier-3 merge** — extension column now spans Tiers 2–3 (17 records). Regenerate whenever a record changes. Not legal advice; `[VERIFY]` flags carry through from records.

**The rule:** build to each row's floor and the product satisfies every Tier-1 regime on that axis *by construction*. Where a Tier-2 regime is in scope, apply its extension note. Each floor cites its setter.

| Axis | Design floor | Floor-setter | Tier-2 extension (apply if in scope) |
|---|---|---|---|

**Sectoral overlays (v1.1):** when a sectoral trigger fires, that record's S3 map rewrites the affected floors for the triggered scope — HIPAA hardens A3/A5/A8/A11 for PHI (60-day clock, minimum-necessary, 18 identifiers CRITICAL); GLBA sets a 2-year disposal deadline and prescriptive encryption+MFA for NPI; COPPA replaces A9 for US under-13 data; BIPA imposes written-release + statutory retention schedule + class-action exposure math for Illinois biometrics; **ePrivacy makes consent-before-fire the only basis for terminal-equipment access; NIS2 adds a 24h incident early-warning; DORA's 4-hour major-incident clock is the tightest in the suite; the AI Act extends A3 minimization to training corpora; FERPA swaps in its inspect/amend rights model for education records.** See the `regulatory-taxonomy--us-*` / `--eu-{eprivacy,nis2,dora,ai-act}` sectoral records.

**Why this matrix is cheap to satisfy:** most floors are `ARCH-DISSOLVES`/`ARCH-SATISFIES` obligations (see `regulatory-taxonomy--arch-rollup.md`) — a selective-disclosure design meets A3/A5/A7/A8-trigger/A10 by construction, leaving the procedural floors (notices, rights workflow, PIA paper) as the actual work.
